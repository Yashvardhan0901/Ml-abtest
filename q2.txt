# Business Interpretation Report

A CNN-based image classification model was developed to automate the categorization of fashion product images uploaded by vendors. The solution analyzes product images and predicts the appropriate product category, reducing the need for manual catalog management. The model learns visual characteristics such as shape, color, texture, and design patterns to distinguish between different fashion products.

The analysis showed that most products can be automatically categorized with high confidence, while a smaller number of images require manual verification due to uncertainty in predictions. This approach helps improve catalog consistency, reduce human effort, and accelerate product onboarding.

# Model Performance Report

A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) was used for multi-class image classification. To reduce computational complexity and training time, the dataset was sampled to include the top product categories and a representative subset of images.

The model was trained on 4,000 images and evaluated on 1,000 unseen test images. CNN was selected because it is highly effective in extracting visual features from image data. The model generated prediction probabilities for each category, and the highest probability was considered the confidence score.

Images with confidence scores below 80% were flagged for manual verification to ensure catalog quality and reduce the risk of incorrect product classification.

# Decision-Making Summary

The developed solution enables automated decision-making for catalog management:

1. High-confidence predictions (≥ 80%) are automatically categorized and added to the product catalog.
2. Low-confidence predictions (< 80%) are routed for manual verification.
3. The system reduces manual workload while maintaining catalog accuracy.
4. Product categories are assigned consistently using learned image features.
5. The confidence-based review mechanism improves reliability and quality control.

# Final Recommendation

Deploy the CNN-based image classification system as an automated catalog management solution. High-confidence predictions should be processed automatically, while low-confidence predictions should be reviewed by catalog management teams.

This hybrid approach will:

* Reduce manual cataloging effort.
* Improve catalog accuracy and consistency.
* Accelerate product onboarding.
* Enhance customer experience through better product organization.
* Support scalable management of millions of vendor-uploaded product images.

Overall, the proposed solution provides an efficient and scalable framework for automated fashion catalog management while maintaining quality through confidence-based manual verification.
